## Two Pointers Technique
The Two-Pointers Technique is a simple yet powerful strategy where you use two indices (pointers) that traverse a data structure - such as an array, list, or string - either toward each other or in the same direction to solve problems more efficiently
Two pointers is really an easy and effective technique that is typically used for Two Sum in Sorted Arrays, Closest Two Sum, Three Sum, Four Sum, Trapping Rain Water and many other popular

Use two indices to iterate through an array/string in a controlled way to reduce time complexity from O(n²) → O(n).
### Where Is It Used?

You should immediately think of Two Pointers when:
```md
Array is sorted

Problem asks for pair

Problem involves palindrome

Remove duplicates

Partitioning

In-place modifications
```
